## 1. Definition: Linear Combination (선형 결합)

Let $V$ be a vector space and $S$ be a nonempty subset of $V$. A vector $v \in V$ is called a **linear combination** of vectors of $S$ if there exist a finite number of vectors $u_1, u_2, \dots, u_n \in S$ and scalars $a_1, a_2, \dots, a_n \in F$ such that:

$$
v = a_1 u_1 + a_2 u_2 + \dots + a_n u_n
$$

In this case, we say $v$ is a linear combination of $u_1, \dots, u_n$.

### Example 2 (Polynomials)
Let $u_1 = x^3 - 2x^2 - 5x - 3$ and $u_2 = 3x^3 - 5x^2 - 4x - 9$ in $P_3(\mathbb{R})$.

**(1) Is $v = 2x^3 - 2x^2 + 12x - 6$ a linear combination of $u_1, u_2$?**
We check if $v = au_1 + bu_2$ has a solution.
$$
2x^3 - 2x^2 + 12x - 6 = (a+3b)x^3 + (-2a-5b)x^2 + (-5a-4b)x + (-3a-9b)
$$
Comparing coefficients:
$$
\begin{cases}
a + 3b = 2 \\
-2a - 5b = -2 \\
-5a - 4b = 12 \\
-3a - 9b = -6
\end{cases}
$$
Solving this system yields $a = -4, b = 2$.
Since a solution exists, it **is** a linear combination.

**(2) Is $w = 3x^3 - 2x^2 + x + 8$ a linear combination of $u_1, u_2$?**
$$
3x^3 - 2x^2 + x + 8 = a u_1 + b u_2
$$
Comparing coefficients:
$$
\begin{cases}
a + 3b = 3 \\
-2a - 5b = -2 \\
\dots
\end{cases}
$$
Solving for the first two equations gives specific $a, b$, but they do not satisfy the remaining equations (e.g., $-5a - 4b \neq 1$).
Since there is **no solution**, it is **NOT** a linear combination.

## 2. Definition: Span (생성)

Let $S$ be a nonempty subset of a vector space $V$. The **span of $S$**, denoted by $span(S)$, is the set of all linear combinations of the vectors in $S$.

$$
span(S) = \{ a_1 u_1 + \dots + a_n u_n : n \in \mathbb{N}, a_i \in F, u_i \in S \}
$$

**Example:**
$$
span\{(1,0,0), (0,1,0)\} = \{ (a,b,0) : a,b \in \mathbb{R} \}
$$
This represents the $xy$-plane in $\mathbb{R}^3$.

## 3. Theorem 1.5: Properties of Span

The span of any subset $S$ of a vector space $V$ is a **subspace** of $V$ that contains $S$. Moreover, any subspace of $V$ that contains $S$ must also contain the span of $S$. (i.e., $span(S)$ is the **smallest** subspace containing $S$.)

### Proof
**(1) $span(S)$ is a subspace of $V$:**
* **Zero vector:** If $S = \emptyset$, $span(\emptyset) = \{0\}$. If $S \neq \emptyset$, $0 = 0u_1 + \dots \in span(S)$.
* **Closed under Addition & Scalar Multiplication:**
    Let $x, y \in span(S)$ and $c \in F$.
    $$
    x = \sum_{i=1}^{m} a_i u_i, \quad y = \sum_{j=1}^{n} b_j v_j \quad (u_i, v_j \in S)
    $$
    Then, $x + y$ is a sum of vectors in $S$, so $x+y \in span(S)$.
    Also, $cx = \sum (ca_i) u_i \in span(S)$.
    $\therefore span(S)$ is a subspace.

**(2) Minimal Property:**
Let $W$ be any subspace of $V$ containing $S$.
Since $W$ is a subspace, it is closed under addition and scalar multiplication. Thus, any linear combination of vectors in $S$ must be in $W$.
$$
\therefore span(S) \subseteq W
$$

## 4. Definition: Generating Sets (생성 집합)

A subset $S$ of a vector space $V$ **generates** (or spans) $V$ if $span(S) = V$.

### Examples
1.  **$\mathbb{R}^3$:** The set $\{(1,1,0), (1,0,1), (0,1,1)\}$ spans $\mathbb{R}^3$.
    For any $(a_1, a_2, a_3) \in \mathbb{R}^3$, we can find scalars $r, s, t$ such that:
    $$
    r(1,1,0) + s(1,0,1) + t(0,1,1) = (a_1, a_2, a_3)
    $$
    Solving the system gives unique solutions for $r, s, t$.

2.  **$P_2(\mathbb{R})$:** The polynomials $\{x^2+3x-2, 2x^2+5x-3, -x^2-4x+4\}$ generate $P_2(\mathbb{R})$.

3.  **Matrices:** The set of three matrices (Example 5 in note) does **NOT** generate $M_{2 \times 2}(\mathbb{R})$ (since dimension is 4, we need at least 4 matrices).

## 5. Geometric Interpretation

The plane generated by two vectors $u, v$ in $\mathbb{R}^3$ consists of all vectors $x$ of the form:

$$
x = tu + sv \quad (t, s \in \mathbb{R})
$$

$x \in \mathbb{R}^3$ is a linear combination of $u, v$ $\iff$ $x$ lies in the plane containing $u$ and $v$ (and the origin).
